## Q1 (RECALL - Foundation)
**Question:** In the context of productivity, what is the primary difference between output and outcome?

**A:** Output refers to the quantity of activities completed, while outcome refers to the actual results or value achieved.

**B:** Output is always more important than outcome.

**C:** There is no meaningful difference between output and outcome in productivity.

**D:** Output measures time spent, while outcome measures money earned.

**Correct Answer:** A

**Why:** This directly tests the core concept taught in the lesson - the fundamental distinction that underpins all productivity thinking.

**Quality Check:** ✅ Standalone (doesn't require other questions), ✅ Native English, ✅ Clear & unambiguous

## Q4 (APPLICATION - Rewritten from Definition Focus)
**Question:** A project manager realizes they spend 4 hours daily in meetings but struggles to meet deadlines. Using the lesson's framework, what does this scenario suggest about their productivity problem?

**A:** They have too many constraints to manage

**B:** Their output (meetings) is high, but their outcome (completed work) is low - indicating misaligned effort

**C:** They need to attend more meetings to improve productivity

**D:** Their energy constraints make meetings impossible

**Correct Answer:** B

**Why:** This rewrites the definition-based question into an APPLICATION question about diagnosing a real productivity problem. Students must understand the formula: Productivity = Outcome / Constraints, and apply it to a workplace situation.

**Quality Check:** ✅ Requires deeper thinking, ✅ Tests APPLICATION not just recall, ✅ Realistic business scenario, ✅ Intelligent answer options

## Q5 (APPLICATION - Scenario)
**Question:** If someone's personal constraint is limited attention span (can focus deeply for only 2 hours daily), which approach better reflects the lesson's productivity formula?

**A:** Schedule 8 hours of tasks requiring deep focus

**B:** Prioritize high-impact tasks that require deep focus into those 2 focused hours, and delegate or defer other work

**C:** Avoid tasks requiring deep focus entirely

**D:** Extend working hours to compensate for short attention span

**Correct Answer:** B

**Why:** Tests APPLICATION of the outcome/constraint formula. Students must understand that productivity isn't about working more - it's about optimizing within real constraints.

**Quality Check:** ✅ Tests practical application, ✅ Forces recognition of constraints as features, not bugs, ✅ Teaches strategic thinking

## Q6 (APPLICATION - NEW - Practice/Decision Making)
**Question:** An engineer spent 6 hours completing a comprehensive technical document (output) but the document's key findings were lost in excessive detail and weren't read by decision-makers (low outcome). According to the lesson's logic, which is the most productive response?

**A:** Spend 8 hours next time to make the document even more comprehensive

**B:** Accept that this type of work has inherently low productivity

**C:** Create a 1-hour summary highlighting only critical findings that decision-makers will actually use and act on

**D:** Stop creating technical documents entirely

**Correct Answer:** C

**Why:** This NEW question combines multiple lesson concepts: output vs outcome distinction, constraint recognition (time), and strategic decision-making. Forces students to think about how to achieve better OUTCOMES within the same or fewer constraints.

**Quality Check:** ✅ NEW original question, ✅ Multifaceted (combines several concepts), ✅ Tests real-world judgment, ✅ Answers show intelligent alternatives

## Q7 (CRITICAL THINKING - NEW - Synthesis & Analysis)
**Question:** If someone's productivity formula produces an outcome ratio of 15 positive customer interactions (outcome) within 40 weekly work hours (constraint), and they want to increase this ratio to 20 per week, which strategy directly addresses the lesson's productivity definition?

**A:** Work 60 hours per week instead of 40

**B:** Attend more customer meetings (increase output activities)

**C:** Focus on the 5 most valuable customer interactions per week and streamline processes to handle them better with the existing 40 hours

**D:** Increase the number of different tasks performed daily

**Correct Answer:** C

**Why:** This CRITICAL THINKING question requires synthesis. Students must understand that productivity = outcome/constraints, then work backward to solve for better productivity. Multiple viable sounding answers exist, but only one truly understands the lesson's core: maximize outcome while respecting (or even leveraging) constraints.

**Quality Check:** ✅ CRITICAL THINKING level, ✅ Requires understanding the formula, ✅ Tests synthesis of multiple concepts, ✅ Shows difference between smart vs naive optimization
